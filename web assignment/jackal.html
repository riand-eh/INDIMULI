Black-backed Jackal

The black-backed jackal is a member of the dog family, recognized by its rusty-red coat, distinctive black back patch, and black-tipped tail. It measures about 95 cm (37 inches) from head to tail.

These opportunistic omnivores hunt small antelopes, feed on insects, reptiles, birds, fruits, and scavenge on carcasses left by larger predators.

They are widely distributed across East and southern Africa, thriving in deserts, grasslands, and savannahs.

The black-backed jackal is listed as "least concern" by the IUCN but faces threats from farmers protecting livestock.
