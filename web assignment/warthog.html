Desert Warthog

The desert warthog is a member of the pig family, easily identified by its large, warty head, a coarse mane, and two pairs of tusks.

Unlike the common warthog, which can be found across most of Africa, the desert warthog is only found in Kenya, Ethiopia, and Somalia. It inhabits savannah and shrubland habitats, where it feeds on grass and other vegetation and shelters in abandoned aardvark burrows.

The desert warthog is listed as "least concern" by the IUCN. However, populations in Kenya face challenges due to increasing human and livestock numbers, which create competition for water and food.
