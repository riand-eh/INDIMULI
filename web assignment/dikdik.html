Dik-dik

Dik-diks are tiny antelopes, perfectly suited to the arid regions of East Africa. These adorable creatures rely on the water content in plants to survive, making them experts at thriving in dry habitats.

They use their small size and dense undergrowth to hide from predators like eagles and cats. Kenya hosts two species of dik-diks:

  • Kirk’s dik-dik: The larger of the two, reaching a height of about 45 cm (18 inches).
  • Guenther’s dik-dik: Known for its petite size and a longer snout.

Although their conservation status is listed as "least concern" by the IUCN, threats such as expanding agriculture and overhunting in certain areas continue to challenge their survival.
