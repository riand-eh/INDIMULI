White-bellied Go-away Bird

The white-bellied go-away bird gets its name from its distinctive call, which sounds like "go-way." This bird is characterized by its grey plumage, a white belly, and a large crest on the top of its head. It also features white markings on its tail, which are visible in flight.

These birds inhabit the arid savannahs and woodlands of East Africa, including Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, Sudan, South Sudan, Somalia, and Ethiopia.

According to the IUCN, the white-bellied go-away bird is listed as "least concern," with no significant threats to its survival.
