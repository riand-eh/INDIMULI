Hyenas

There are two species of hyenas in Kenya—the striped hyena and the spotted hyena. They inhabit most of Africa, except for rainforests.

The spotted hyena, also known as the "laughing hyena," is the largest species. Females are larger than males, and their coats feature distinctive dark spots. These versatile predators hunt fish, birds, and even hippo calves. Working in packs, they can take down larger prey such as wildebeests or antelopes.

Striped hyenas, though lesser known, are primarily scavengers. They prefer to come out at night and feed on carcasses left behind by other predators.

The IUCN classifies the spotted hyena as "least concern," while the striped hyena is listed as "near threatened." Human-wildlife conflicts pose a significant threat to their survival.
